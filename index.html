About Me

My Journey in Medicine

Hi, I'm Kedarnath Reddy, a medical student from India with a passion for surgery and global healthcare. I love the blend of creativity, precision, and problem-solving that surgery demands, and I’ve been fortunate to gain hands-on experience across diverse specialties while contributing to international research projects. Outside the hospital, you’ll usually find me running long distances, playing tennis or swimming, or exploring new places. For me, medicine is not just a career—it’s a way to connect, learn, and make a real difference in people’s lives.

From the first time I held a scalpel during an anatomy dissection, I felt a profound thrill—the union of art and science, the precise choreography of hand and mind. My parents and grandfather, all into science, nurtured my curiosity in physics and chemistry, but it was the living canvas of the human body that truly captivated me. I still hear my anatomy professor’s words echoing in my mind: “You will become a good surgeon one day.” In that moment, I knew I had found my calling. As a child, I was always building, repairing, and experimenting- my room often looked more like a laboratory than a bedroom. I was fascinated by design and function, by understanding how things work and how to make them better. That curiosity naturally evolved when I entered medical school. The soldering iron was replaced by surgical instruments, and circuits gave way to sutures. From placing a drain to holding a needle at the right angle, I saw the principles of physics—Poiseuille’s Law, Laplace’s Law—come alive in the operating room. Surgery, to me, wasn’t just a skill; it was the perfect intersection of logic, creativity, and human impact.
